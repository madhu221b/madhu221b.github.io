I had a full life, <br/>
A son, two daughters and a wife.<br/>
Witnessing the drudgery of my father, <br/>
I grew up,<br/>
With only one determination,<br/>
I won't sweat in a farm,<br/>
I would sweat in the dim light of the study,<br/>
The study in a cow dung patted hut,<br/>
Education upheld me,<br/>
Pulled me from the grasps of poverty,<br/>
When I retired,<br/>
I didnt retire too.<br/>
I worked more for 15 years,<br/>
For why be at ease, when your mind is not.<br/>
You could always find me,<br/>
Cooped up with a magnifying glass,<br/>
And those tiny lettered insurance documents.<br/>
I then shivered with a paralysis attack.<br/>
My legs didn't walk for me like they used to,<br/>
I was tended to and taken care of,<br/>
No, that wasn't a luxury for me,<br/>
For I an irresolute man,<br/>
Depending on someone to feed and bathe me,<br/>
My mind lost the battle now,<br/>
And I coughed up the soup and I finally rested.<br/>
Lived to see the fruits of hard work,<br/>
Lived to see that what I believed took me ahead<br/>
— <i>Madhura Pawar</i>
