---
title: The Crumbled Bricks
tags: [Poetry]
style: fill
color: light
description: An attempt to express the hardship faced by migrant workers due to lockdown .
---

He enjoyed when the water & cement,<br/>
Mixed with a shade of grey faint,<br/>
Seeing the red,bright bricks lowering from the truck,<br/>
Determined he set to work till 5 pm struck.<br/>

Known for his speed & skill,<br/>
Building walls his everyday’s drill,<br/>
Renowned as the ‘Bolt’ on the site,<br/>
Modest Balu laid bricks , all day with might.<br/>

Diligent Balu , apple of eye of the Sahab,<br/>
‘कभी कुछ लगा तो बोलना Balu’ , told the Sahab.<br/>
As he patted the skinny bricklayer,<br/>
Looking at Balu’s work , he walked away happier.<br/>

But one day, came a news through the supervisor,<br/>
Which shook Balu so bad,<br/>
That his tools slipped through his hand and hurt his foot,<br/>
Numbed by the pain , his future now moot.<br/>

“Site will be closed.No daily wages”,<br/>
These words echoed in his ear,<br/>
Sleeping empty stomach was not his fear,<br/>
It was starving the ones he held dear.<br/>

His frail wife &  6 months old baby ,<br/>
Is all he could picture ,<br/>
He dashed towards Sahab’s Sedan,<br/>
A teary eyed Balu pleaded,<br/>
For without the money ,his family would be dead.<br/>

Sahab , now imitating as if talking on phone,<br/>
Motioning the drive to drive away,<br/>
Leaving Balu helpless and alone,<br/>
Trudging back , to his home he made way.<br/>

His wife stood with her palm open,<br/>
So Balu could give her today’s money.<br/>
So that she could buy some flour & oil from ration store,<br/>
But now she observed the dejected look her husband bore.<br/>

Balu entering the hut kissed the baby,<br/>
His eyes moist & wet now,<br/>
His wife ,with her saari, wiped his brow,<br/>
Whispering to him ,”God will look after us somehow”<br/>

— <i>Madhura Pawar</i>
