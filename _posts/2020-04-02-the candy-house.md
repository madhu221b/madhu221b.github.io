title: The Candy House

“Stuff as many candies as you can & hurry” <br/>
And from our granny’s jar into our pockets went,<br/>
Hersheys, M&Ms and a lot of gummies ,<br/>
The two kids now looking like pregnant ladies.<br/>

And like that,with our loot, we tiptoed,<br/>
For getting discovered was the fright,<br/>
Hence we trod very light,<br/>
Out of the house now, we yelped with delight.<br/>

The “base” for devouring the loot,<br/>
Was the ruined establishment opposite,<br/>
Just a 10 m road stood between,<br/>
From us & the “safe haven” of ours.<br/>

The gate,once painted peacock blue,<br/>
Rusted with age stood broken,<br/>
Entering the gate,emerged a barren pathway,<br/>
Seemingly not at all lively & gay.<br/>

At the end of that trail,<br/>
Stood a cabin , bleak & frail,<br/>
Time had deprived it of its paint,<br/>
Now it stood alone,lifeless & quaint.<br/>

Emptying our stuffed pockets,<br/>
The candy loot now on the ground,<br/>
A transient resident it remained of the cabin’s porch,<br/>
For we went on to gorge<br/>

Our insatiable appetite for chocolates now pacified,<br/>
We looked around to find all the trees worn & dried,<br/>
Meandering around the cabin,<br/>
To get inside we both now keen.<br/>

This old habitation , what could it be ?<br/>
Why is it devoid of all the glee ?<br/>
What could have been its past ?<br/>
These questions , how long would they last ?<br/>


The huge , wooden door won’t budge,<br/>
But then we discovered a keyhole,<br/>
Round & circular , it was a thing , now opportune,<br/>
The hole , now answer to the place’s misfortune.<br/>

And in we peeped & witnessed a bizarre sight,<br/>
A wooden coffin like structure stood there upright,<br/>
The walls seemed to have been blazed,<br/>
With ash & cinders, they glazed.<br/>

Trudging back we tried to wonder,<br/>
Of the past this place might hold .<br/>
The tale still remains untold,<br/>
Which could be the real one among our theories so manifold.<br/>

I wish I had the ability ,<br/>
To speak to the dilapidated place,<br/>
I feel it screaming , wanting to tell us,<br/>
How it once had all the life and fuss.<br/>
  


— <i>Madhura Pawar</i>
