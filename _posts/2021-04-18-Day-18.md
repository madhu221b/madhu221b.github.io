The magic biscuit tin,<br/>
Our aim to devour it clean,<br/>
Kept on the topmost kitchen shelf,<br/>
To access it without any grownup's help.<br/>
<br/>
The tin - made of thin metal sheets, <br/>
With the lid locking it secure,<br/>
A difficult task for tiny fingers of a kid,<br/>
A naughty mind but the hands are timid.<br/>
<br/>
And when the lid opened,<br/>
What an assortment,<br/>
The sweet, faint smell of biscuits,<br/>
An aroma never to be forgotten.<br/>
<br/>
Chocolate chip cookies,<br/>
The salted rectangular biscuits,<br/>
The crunchy ones in the shape of animals,<br/>
And the coconut flavoured ones.<br/>
<br/>
Karachi biscuits which greased the tin most,<br/>
Corn flakes covered, crunchy ones, <br/>
Dry fruit cookies,<br/>
Topped with slices of almonds.<br/>
<br/>
The tin now obsolete, <br/>
Yet the memory remains, <br/>
Of it siting on the top, <br/>
With its diverse contents.<br/>
— <i>Madhura Pawar</i>
